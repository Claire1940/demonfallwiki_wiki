<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Movement & Parkour Tutorial">

Vertical mobility is a core aspect of world exploration in Demonfall. Players often need to scale tall cliffs, navigate rooftops, and climb mountain ranges to reach breathing trainers, hidden NPCs, or quest objectives. Because the standard double jump is insufficient for scaling tall vertical walls, players must master the wall-climb chain mechanic.

Using shift lock is highly recommended during parkour. Locking your camera makes it much easier to control your character's orientation as you bounce between vertical surfaces.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Shift Lock">
    Press LB and RB simultaneously (on controller) or the Shift key (on keyboard) to lock your camera perspective. This ensures your character always faces the direction of your camera.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Jump at the Wall">
    Run toward the vertical surface and press the jump button (A on Xbox, X on PlayStation, Spacebar on PC) to make initial contact with the wall.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Rotate and Jump Again">
    While touching the wall, quickly rotate your camera to face the opposite wall or angle, then press the jump button again to bounce upward.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Chain Jumps to the Top">
    Repeat the camera-rotation and jump sequence in rapid succession. This allows you to climb incredibly high walls without sliding back down.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Movement Stamina">
Wall climbing does not consume your breathing meter, but dashing does. Ensure your stamina bar is fully charged before attempting long-distance parkour sequences to avoid falling mid-climb.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival & Inventory Management">

Survival in Demonfall requires careful management of your status bars. In addition to health and stamina, players must closely monitor their hunger meter. If your hunger meter empties, your character's natural health regeneration stops completely, making you highly vulnerable to any incoming damage.

Managing your inventory efficiently ensures you can quickly recover during or after intense combat encounters.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Slayer Survival">
    Slayers rely heavily on purchasing soup from village vendors. Consuming soup restores your hunger meter and enables continuous health regeneration during long PvE farming sessions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Demon Survival">
    Demons must feed on defeated human NPCs or players to satisfy their hunger. If a demon's hunger bar empties, they cannot regenerate health, leading to swift defeat in combat.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Inventory Hotbars">
    Items must be equipped to the Z and X slots from your bag menu. Use the triggers (RT/LT) to highlight these slots and press the interaction button to consume items like soup mid-fight.
  </Card>
