<Section icon={Trophy} title="Yen Farming: The Spawn Trinket Rotation Route">

Gathering 4,400 Yen can feel daunting for new players, but following a disciplined trinket farming rotation makes the process quick and safe. Trinkets like rings, cups, and necklaces spawn dynamically across the starting village. By utilizing a specific room-by-room search pattern and resetting your character to loop the spawns, you can secure the necessary funds in under an hour.

The key to this method is speed and map knowledge. Trinkets frequently spawn on furniture inside residential buildings, tucked away in dark alleyways, and behind environmental obstacles like trees and stone walls. Master this route to optimize your farming efficiency.

<Callout type="tip" title="Server Hopping Tip">
If you find a server where no trinkets are spawning, the lobby may be over-farmed. Simply hop to a lower-population server to find fresh spawns across all designated locations.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Spawn Room Search">
    Open your menu, navigate to configuration, and reset your character. You will spawn inside a residential room. Immediately check the desk, the bed, and the shelves for cups or rings, then press E to collect them.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Downstairs & Outer Walls">
    Head downstairs and inspect the lower shelves. Exit the building, turn left, and jump over the stone wall. Check the three corners of the immediate courtyard and search the base of the large tree for hidden items.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Alleys and Barrels">
    Leap over the adjacent wall to check the backyard. Head into the narrow alleys, looking specifically under wooden planks. Climb the nearby house and check the wooden barrels sitting behind the building.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Sell and Reset">
    Once your inventory is full of trinkets, head to the local merchant to sell your haul. Reset your character to instantly teleport back to the starting spawn room and repeat the entire loop.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Thunder Breathing Moveset & Skill Tree">

Thunder Breathing features five active offensive forms that prioritize speed, shock value, and quick combo resets. It relies on gap-closers that catch opponents off guard, making it highly effective for players who enjoy an aggressive, rush-down playstyle. To utilize this style effectively, you must understand the utility of each form.

The cornerstone of the moveset is Thunderclap and Flash, which allows you to teleport forward and strike your opponent instantly. When combined with the stun from Heat Lightning, you can lock opponents into long, inescapable damage windows.

<Callout type="info" title="Breath Bar Management">
Thunder Breathing moves consume a significant portion of your Breath Bar. Always weave basic M1 attacks between your skills to recharge your meter and maintain pressure.
</Callout>

| Form Name | Keybind | Breath Cost | Combat Utility & Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Thunderclap & Flash** | Z | Low | A lightning-fast dash that auto-targets and strikes the nearest foe. |
| **Heat Lightning** | X | Medium | A localized lightning strike that causes high block-break stun. |
| **Thunder Swarm** | C | Medium | An area-of-effect slash flurry that repels multiple surrounding enemies. |
| **Distant Thunder** | V | High | A ranged lightning projectile that strikes targets from afar. |
| **Rice Spirit** | B | High | A defensive counter-attack that strikes back when hit by an enemy. |
