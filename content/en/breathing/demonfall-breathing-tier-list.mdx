<Section icon={Swords} title="S-Tier: The Pinnacle of Combat">

The S-Tier is reserved for styles that can end a fight in a single well-executed combo. In 2026, Sun and Moon Breathing continue to dominate the high-rank PvP leaderboards and are the preferred choices for speed-running the Infinity Castle.

### Sun Breathing
Sun Breathing is arguably the strongest offensive style in Demonfall. It features massive hitboxes and a "Burn" effect that prevents enemies from regenerating health effectively. However, it is a late-game style that drains stamina rapidly, requiring players to have a high level and ideally the Kamado clan for the stamina reduction buff.

### Moon Breathing
Moon Breathing excels at mid-to-long range. Its attacks create crescent moon projectiles that make it incredibly difficult for opponents to close the distance. It is the go-to choice for players who prefer a "Zoning" playstyle, keeping enemies at bay while chipping away at their health.

| Style | Key Advantage | Best Synergy | Skill Floor |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sun** | Highest raw damage per hit | Kamado Clan | Extreme |
| **Moon** | Exceptional range and pressure | Tsugikuni Clan | High |

<Section icon={Shield} title="A-Tier: Versatile and Reliable">

Styles in the A-Tier are the backbone of the Demonfall community. They are powerful enough to clear all content but don't require the extreme RNG or grind associated with S-Tier styles.

### Water Breathing
Often dismissed as a "starter" style, Water Breathing is actually one of the most consistent performers in the game. It provides excellent defensive options, including moves that offer invincibility frames (iframes), allowing you to dodge boss ultimates with ease. It is the gold standard for consistency.

### Thunder Breathing
Thunder Breathing is all about speed. Its primary move, Thunderclap and Flash, is one of the best gap-closers in the game. In PvP, a skilled Thunder user can be a nightmare to track, darting in and out of combat before the opponent can react.

### Mist Breathing
Mist Breathing focuses on obscuring the user's movements. It features several dash-based attacks that can confuse opponents in PvP. It ranks highly because of its "Seventh Form," which allows for incredible mobility and unpredictability.

| Style | PvP Rating | PvE Rating | Core Mechanic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Water** | 4/5 | 5/5 | Defensive Counters |
| **Thunder** | 5/5 | 3/5 | Extreme Speed |
| **Mist** | 4/5 | 4/5 | Mobility/Dash |

<Callout type="info" title="Why Water is A-Tier">

<Section icon={Map} title="How to Unlock Your Breathing Style">

Unlocking a breathing style in Demonfall is a multi-step process that involves finding a specific NPC trainer hidden throughout the map. Most trainers are located in or near major villages like Hayakawa or Okuyia.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Reach Level 10">
    Before you can train in any style, you must prove your worth. Grind early quests in Hayakawa Village until you hit level 10.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Gather Resources">
    Most trainers require a fee of 2,000 to 5,000 Yen. Some, like the Mist trainer, may require specific items or tasks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locate the Trainer">
    Travel to the trainer's location. For example, Urokodaki (Water) is found near the coast, while the Thunder trainer is located on a high plateau.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Complete the Trial">
    Trainers will set a challenge, such as a parkour course or defeating a specific number of demons. Complete this to unlock the base style.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Invest Skill Points">
    Open your skill tree and spend points to unlock the various "Forms" of your chosen breathing style.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Breath Indict Warning">
If you wish to change your breathing style later, you will need a **Breath Indict**. These are rare drops or can be purchased with Robux/Codes. Choose your first style carefully!
</Callout>

</Section>
