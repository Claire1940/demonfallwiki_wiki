<Section icon={Target} title="B-Tier & Specialist Breathing Options">

B-Tier styles are the backbone of the **demonfall best breathing** list for mid-game players and specialists. These styles are balanced—neither overpowered nor weak—and often excel in niche roles like Raids or support.

Water Breathing is the gold standard for balance. It is often recommended for beginners because its moves are straightforward and teach the fundamentals of timing and spacing. While it doesn't have the flashy "one-shot" potential of Moon, it provides consistent damage and reliable defensive options.

Wind Breathing has recently been buffed, earning the nickname "The New Moon" among veteran players. While it lacks true combo extenders, it deals massive raw damage. It is arguably the best breathing for Raids and the Infinity Castle because its long-range gusts can gather and control large groups of NPCs simultaneously.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Thunder Breathing">
    Focus on speed. This style is "brain dead" in its efficiency, using fast-paced moves with no wind-up. However, be wary of bugs that can occasionally cause moves to miss.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Serpent Breathing">
    Master the 1v1. Serpent requires high skill to land its tricky combo extenders, but its ultimate is one of the strongest combo enders in the game.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Love Breathing">
    Utilize speed and support. Love breathing moves have almost no wind-up and can even heal teammates, making it the premier choice for group play.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Lower Tiers: Niche and Beginner Styles">

The remaining styles—Insect, Sound, and Stone—fall into the lower tiers not because they are "bad," but because they are highly situational or outclassed by the A and S tiers. Every breathing style is viable if mastered, but these require significantly more effort to achieve the same results as a Moon or Sun user.

Insect Breathing features the fastest M1 attacks in the game, making it nearly impossible for enemies to shoulder bash you. However, it suffers in PvE because NPC AI often ignores the subtle stun mechanics of the style. Sound Breathing deals high damage but suffers from long wind-up animations that experienced players can easily dodge or parry. Stone Breathing provides excellent stun and AoE but feels awkward due to its unique heavy blade animations.

<Section icon={Map} title="How to Obtain and Switch Breathing">

Acquiring the **demonfall best breathing** styles involves finding specific NPCs hidden throughout the world map. Each trainer will require a series of tasks, usually involving a payment of Yen and a combat challenge or a collection quest.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate the Trainer">
    Travel to the specific location for your desired style (e.g., Urokodaki for Water, or the secret locations for Moon/Sun).
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Complete the Quest">
    Trainers will ask you to perform tasks like splitting a boulder, surviving a race, or defeating a mini-boss.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Pay the Fee">
    Most basic breathing styles require 2,000 Yen. Advanced styles like Sun or Moon have much steeper requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Resetting Styles">
    To switch, you must use a Breath Indict. This item resets your current style, allowing you to learn a new one from a different trainer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Requirement Type | Basic Breathing | Advanced (Sun/Moon) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yen Cost** | 2,000 | Varies (High) |
