<Section icon={Swords} title="Demonfall Combat Basics: Core Mechanics & Controls">

Engaging in **demonfall combat** requires precise timing, quick reflexes, and an understanding of spacing. Unlike standard action RPGs, button-mashing will quickly lead to defeat. Every strike, dash, and block must be calculated to prevent your opponent from exploiting your recovery frames. 

The foundation of survival lies in mastering the basic controls. Below is a comprehensive breakdown of the essential inputs required to navigate both PvE and PvP encounters.

| Action | Keybind | Combat Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light Attack** | Left Click | Fast melee strikes to build combos and chip away at enemy posture |
| **Heavy Attack** | Right Click | Slower, high-damage strike used to break guards and knock back foes |
| **Block** | F | Reduces incoming damage, though vulnerable to heavy block-breakers |
| **Dash** | Q | Quick directional movement to evade attacks or close the distance |
| **Breathe** | G | Charges your breathing bar, essential for executing breathing techniques |

Stamina is the lifeblood of your defensive play. Every block and dash consumes stamina. If your stamina bar empties completely while blocking an opponent's heavy attack, you will suffer a posture break, leaving you stunned and exposed to a full combo. 

To prevent posture breaks, you must practice perfect blocking. Pressing the block key right before an enemy attack lands triggers a parry, which stuns the attacker and gives you a brief window to launch a counter-offensive.

<Callout type="warning" title="Guard Breaking Risk">
Heavy attacks (Right Click) are designed to shatter an opponent's block. If you see an enemy charging a heavy attack, do not attempt to block it. Instead, use your dash (Q) to move sideways and punish their recovery frames.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Breathing Styles Tier List: S-Tier to B-Tier Ranked">

For Human and Hybrid players, Breathing Styles are the defining element of your offensive arsenal. These styles grant unique active skills that can chain into basic attack combos.

| Breathing Style | Tier | Combat Focus | Key Move |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sun Breathing** | S-Tier | High burn damage and long combos | Hinokami Kagura |
| **Moon Breathing** | S-Tier | Massive area-of-effect and range | Evening Palace |
| **Water Breathing** | A-Tier | Defensive counters and fluid combos | Constant Flux |
| **Mist Breathing** | A-Tier | High mobility and visual obscuration | Obscuring Clouds |
| **Thunder Breathing** | A-Tier | Rapid dash attacks and burst damage | Rice Spirit |
| **Wind Breathing** | B-Tier | Crowd control and knockbacks | Gale Sudden Gusts |
| **Flame Breathing** | B-Tier | High raw damage but predictable patterns | Purgatory |

### S-Tier Styles
Sun and Moon Breathing represent the pinnacle of swordsmanship. Sun Breathing inflicts a lingering burn status effect that prevents enemies from passive healing, making it exceptionally deadly in long PvP duels. Moon Breathing excels at keeping multiple opponents at bay with wide, sweeping crescent slashes that deal high damage across a large radius.

### A-Tier Styles
Water, Mist, and Thunder Breathing are highly competitive options that perform exceptionally well in most combat scenarios. Water Breathing is the most balanced style, offering excellent defensive counter moves that reset enemy combos. Mist Breathing utilizes clones and teleportation mechanics to disorient opponents, while Thunder Breathing focuses on instantaneous gap-closers to catch running targets.

### B-Tier Styles
Wind and Flame Breathing are solid choices but suffer from predictable animation wind-ups. Wind Breathing is excellent for PvE crowd control, allowing you to gather and knock back groups of enemies. Flame Breathing deals high burst damage, but experienced PvP players can easily parry its highly telegraphed moves.

<Callout type="success" title="Beginner Recommendation">
If you are new to the game, start with Water Breathing. Its defensive moves are highly forgiving, and the training quest is straightforward to complete in the early game.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Endgame Challenges: Boss Fight Strategies">

Defeating endgame bosses is the primary method for acquiring rare crafting materials, Yen, and massive experience points. However, boss encounters feature unique mechanics that can easily wipe unprepared players.

| Boss | Location | Combat Strategy | Recommended Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rui** | Spider Forest | Dodge thread traps, attack during recovery windows | Level 25+ |
| **Akaza** | Central Wastelands | Keep moving to avoid shockwave strikes, parry his punches | Level 40+ |
| **Gyutaro** | Entertainment District | Bring poison resistance, dodge his sweeping sickle moves | Level 45+ |
| **Kaigaku** | Infinity Castle | Space out to avoid lightning strikes, interrupt his casting | Level 50+ |

### Rui Strategy
Rui relies on ranged thread attacks that can bind you in place, followed by a high-damage slam. To defeat him, maintain a medium distance and wait for him to execute his thread-pull move. Dash sideways immediately as the animation begins, then close the distance to land a full light-attack combo.

### Akaza Strategy
Akaza is a hyper-aggressive boss who utilizes rapid fist strikes and explosive shockwaves. Trying to block his entire combo will quickly break your posture. Instead, focus on parrying his initial strike to interrupt his flow, or use high-mobility breathing moves to chip away at his health while backing away.
